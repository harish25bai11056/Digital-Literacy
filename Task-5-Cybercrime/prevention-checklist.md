# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online: Student Prevention Checklist (India Edition)

This checklist provides actionable steps for college students in India to protect themselves against common digital threats, ranging from financial fraud to identity theft.

---

### **Cybersecurity Checklist**

- [ ] **1. Verify UPI "Request Money" Alerts** Never enter your UPI PIN to *receive* money. Scammers send "Request Money" notifications on apps like PhonePe or Google Pay, claiming you’ve won a scholarship or prize. **Authentic payments to you never require your PIN.**

- [ ] **2. Check the Recipient Name before UPI Payment** Before hitting 'Pay' on a QR scan or mobile number, always verify the name displayed on the screen. Fraudsters often use names similar to popular shops or college canteens to trick students into sending money to the wrong account.

- [ ] **3. Use a VPN on College/Public Wi-Fi** Campus and cafe Wi-Fi networks are often unsecured. Use a trusted VPN to encrypt your data. This prevents "Man-in-the-Middle" attacks where hackers intercept your login credentials or private chats.

- [ ] **4. Avoid "Registration Fees" for Internships** Be wary of job or internship offers on WhatsApp or Telegram that ask for a "security deposit," "laptop delivery fee," or "documentation charge." **No legitimate Indian company asks for money to hire you.**

- [ ] **5. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** Activate 2FA on your Gmail, Instagram, and LinkedIn. Even if someone steals your password through a phishing link (like a fake "Vote for me" contest), they cannot access your account without the second code.

- [ ] **6. Be Skeptical of "Urgent" Principal/HOD Messages** A common scam involves receiving a message from a number using your Principal’s or HOD’s photo, asking for urgent help or Amazon gift cards. Always call the person directly on their known number to verify.

- [ ] **7. Never Share OTPs for "Verification"** No bank official or government representative will ever call you to ask for an OTP. If someone claims your SIM card is expiring or your account is blocked and asks for an OTP to "fix" it, hang up immediately.

- [ ] **8. Log Out of Shared Library Computers** If you use computers in the college library or a cyber cafe, never select "Remember Me." Always manually log out and clear the browser history/cache before leaving the terminal.

---

### **🚨 How to Report Cybercrime in India**

If you have been defrauded or harassed online, take immediate action:

* **Financial Fraud:** Call the National Helpline **1930** immediately to report the transaction and attempt to freeze the funds.
* **Official Portal:** File a formal complaint at **[cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)**.
* **Evidence:** Keep screenshots of chats, transaction IDs (UTR numbers), and the fraudster's profile/mobile number.
